austin had 2023 energy this weekend, and not in a "classic americana, great bbq" way. i mean verstappen absolutely demolished the field in a way that felt almost nostalgic (remember when that was just normal for him?). pole position, leading every single lap, checking out mentally by lap 15. the whole thing. oscar piastri's recent form collapse has handed lando norris and max such a clean path back into this championship that we're genuinely looking at a three-way fight that could go the distance.

the buildup was at least entertaining. glen powell showed up for the grid walk alongside martin brundle and actually knew what he was talking about, which is not something you can say about most celebrities at formula 1 (runner up: the young kid who nailed the national anthem without cracking on the high notes). small wins. then the formation lap ended and we all braced for the usual turn 1 demolition job. didn't happen. anticlimactic as hell, but you take it.

the actual racing was split between "surprisingly clean" and "well, there it is." lance stroll quietly threaded through the midfield like a man on a mission (despite his grid penalty for the sprint-race missile he launched at esteban ocon). charles leclerc spent the whole race battling with lando norris and honestly looked competitive. and—get this—ferrari didn't sabotage their own strategy for once. mark this down. call someone. this might be history.

then carlos sainz happened. attempted a move on kimi antonelli that had absolutely no business being attempted, crashed out, and got himself a 5-place grid penalty heading into mexico on top of it. stupid driving, honestly. kimi got punted to the back in the process but at least didn't have to wear the dnf. later, yuki tsunoda and oliver bearman made contact while battling, ollie spun, but clawed his way back to p9. so basically verstappen had a free reign while everyone else sorted themselves out.

the championship math is where things get spicy. piastri's been falling off a cliff. norris has been racking up solid results. verstappen came back from the summer break like a man possessed. those massive point gaps that looked so comfortable for piastri six weeks ago? gone. replaced by a handful of points separating the top three. we're actually looking at this thing going to abu dhabi in december. five races left. five.

mexico city is next weekend, and max's momentum is a freight train right now. norris has to find consistency or he'll get buried. piastri needs to wake up before this championship slips away entirely. it's the tightest three-way fight we've had in years, and it only happened because verstappen remembered how to be verstappen.

see you in mexico.
