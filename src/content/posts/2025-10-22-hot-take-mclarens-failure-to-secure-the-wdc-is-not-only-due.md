let's talk about what should have been a coronation and turned into a thriller nobody asked for.

oscar piastri leads the championship. lando norris is 26 points back. max verstappen is 40 points behind. there are five races and two sprints left. sounds dramatic until you remember that max started this season 104 points down and has basically erased it. that doesn't happen because the car magically got faster. that happens because mclaren forgot how to execute.

the season opened with lando winning the first race, hunting max aggressively in the final laps and breaking his unhinged streak of 1,029 consecutive days leading the championship. at that point, everyone expected a mclaren championship. they had the dominant car (finally), a proper driver pairing, and red bull was clearly vulnerable since adrian newey left for aston martin. the second half of 2024 showed mclaren's performance jump was real. ferrari, mercedes, and mclaren were winning races. red bull's 1-2 dominance from 2023 was over.

first 15 races, the narrative was obvious: piastri and norris would fight it out because they had the better machinery. that's how it should have worked.

then monza happened. max took pole and won (as he does). suddenly, the papaya rules and team orders between oscar and lando started looking less dominant and more like management theater. the real story wasn't the mclaren battle being "intruded upon"—it was that max was just outdriving both of them when it mattered.

here's the actual breakdown. max finished 2nd at melbourne, jeddah, montreal, zandvoort, and marina bay. he won at suzuka, imola, monza, baku, and austin. he has more pole positions than both mclaren drivers and is now tied with lando on wins. meanwhile, lando collected three dnfs (montreal, zandvoort, austin sprint) and oscar grabbed two (baku, austin sprint). those aren't strategic losses or bad luck. those are execution failures that cost them everything.

a 104-point gap doesn't close to 40 because one driver suddenly became incredible (max was always incredible, since adrian newey left). it closes because the other two couldn't finish races or nail strategy calls when they had the quicker car. mclaren had the championship gift-wrapped and handed it to max.

now we're in mexico, and if you didn't know max owns this track, you do now. he's won here in 2017, 2018, 2021, 2022, and 2023. lando has never won in mexico city and has exactly one podium there. oscar has nothing. max won't hesitate to extend this gap even further.

the question isn't whether max deserves to be considered the greatest driver ever (that debate exists, and it's not boring). the question is whether mclaren will actually execute when they need to. they've had every advantage and squandered it. five races left. two sprints. the papaya rules cost them the championship before max even finished closing the gap.

see you in mexico city.
