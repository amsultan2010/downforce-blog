heading into mexico, verstappen needed to be perfect. his championship window was closing and everyone knew it. piastri was still ahead in points, but max had been shrinking the gap consistently. so naturally, the weekend started with ferrari pulling their classic trick of looking genuinely quick during practice (you know, the one that never translates to qualifying). except this time, they actually weren't lying. leclerc topped fp1, hamilton hit second in fp3, and the red cars looked properly quick.

then qualifying happened and ferrari didn't fumble it (mark this down, folks). leclerc p2, hamilton p3 (his best grid slot all season, by the way), norris on pole, russell p4, and verstappen in p5. piastri somehow managed to qualify p8, which felt like a gift wrapped for norris to chip away at the championship lead.

the race kicked off with norris managing his tire strategy, but turn 1 was absolute madness. leclerc, hamilton, and verstappen were three-wide heading in. max locked up, went wide, nearly kissed the wall. you know what came next? george russell threw a tantrum about it (as is tradition). then the stewards came down hard on lewis with a 10-second penalty for running wide.

here's where i lose it. half the grid cut that corner. lewis gets the penalty. make it make sense.

that 10-second stop ruined what could've been his first podium in red. instead, oliver bearman had the drive of his life, running p3 for a solid chunk and holding up a rampant verstappen behind him. bearman finished p4, which is legitimately his best result ever and tied haas' all-time best (that was grosjean, 2019). the kid earned that one.

max worked his way through to p3 while norris cruised to the win, finishing 38 seconds clear of leclerc in p2. piastri limped home p5, which meant norris actually took the championship lead for the first time since april. one point ahead. that's dramatic.

but here's the thing that actually infuriated everyone at the watch party (yes, i was up watching at an ungodly hour from riyadh). with a handful of laps to go, max was genuinely hunting charles down. verstappen got within drs range. this was the race within the race. it was happening.

then carlos sainz spun out in the stadium section and parked his car off track. the fia called a vsc.

a vsc. for a car that was already off track.

that completely neutered the verstappen-leclerc fight that was about to get properly spicy. it's the kind of decision that makes you wonder if anyone in the stewards' room actually watched the previous lap. neutralize the race, kill the drama, declare victory for consistency (except there was no consistency with the lewis penalty, so where's that logic coming from).

this is the real issue. the fia travels around the world with a rotating circus of stewards who apparently don't talk to each other between races. one weekend you get something, the next weekend you get the opposite. it's indefensible. they need a permanent panel. just rotate them through races, but keep the same group making the calls. right now it's chaos, and not the fun kind.

so norris leads the championship by one point heading to brazil. verstappen is still max verstappen though, which means expect him to do something ridiculous and win from p17 again (he did it last year, starting 17th and winning). the mclarens will probably run wide into the grass at some point since that's just their brand down there. and lance stroll will either bin it immediately or have the drive of his career depending on which universe we're living in. the forecast is rainy, which means all bets are off.

see you at interlagos.
