são paulo is genuinely one of the last proper circuits on this calendar. it rains almost every year. drivers crash constantly. and for some reason it always matters for the championship (2007, 2008, 2012, 2024... the pattern is real). last year Max Verstappen qualified 12th, got a penalty down to 17th, and then systematically dismantled Lando Norris's title hopes while in what everyone called a slower car. which is why this year, with brazil as one of six sprint races, we all showed up expecting more chaos. and look, the circuit didn't disappoint (even if the weather did sort of bail).

qualifying went Norris on pole, Antonelli second (his best ever grid position, which is legitimately nice to see), Verstappen sixth, and Fernando Alonso fifth (the only fact that matters here, since he's the GOAT and i'm right about this). Piastri was way back in eighth and about to become very relevant in a negative way.

the sprint started on a damp track. so damp that Pirelli's wet tires apparently just didn't exist (which tracks since Pirelli can't make enough tires to save their lives, full stop). everyone was on mediums or softs. Norris led. Antonelli was genuinely rapid behind him. Verstappen moved to fourth. Alonso held fifth and got himself into a little DRS train with Leclerc, Hamilton, Stroll, and Gasly trailing behind like ducks.

then the lap where everything went sideways.

Norris hit a kerb filled with standing water like he was playing mario kart and kicked up a proper tidal wave. Colapinto, Piastri, and Hulkenberg all three aquaplaned into the barriers within seconds of each other. Antonelli's chase got neutralized. the red flag came out. Colapinto and Piastri DNF'd. Hulkenberg limped back to the pits and somehow got repaired in time (respect to the crew).

restart. Norris still led. Kimi Antonelli now had to defend from Russell. and then something genuinely weird happened.

Alonso got LICO'd. not a typo. he was just... told to go slower. maybe Aston Martin found some ferrari upgrades over the break, i don't know. Alonso stayed ahead of Leclerc for about 10 laps before dropping to sixth. you can't make this up (actually the team can, and did).

the chaos continued. Liam Lawson punted Ollie Bearman off the track during an overtake attempt (and before anyone defends him, this is like his fifth incident this season where it's clearly his fault). Pierre Gasly finally overtook the seemingly immovable Lance Stroll for eighth and the last point.

final lap. Gabriel Bortoleto (brazilian native, hometown pressure a real thing) crashed spectacularly into turn 1, nearly took out Alex Albon, crashed again on the right-hand side, but walked away. Albon's car got decorated with a chunk of Nico Hulkenberg's front wing and dropped back. safety car. done.

Norris won. Antonelli second (proper performance that). Russell third. Verstappen, Leclerc, Alonso, Hamilton, and Gasly in the remaining points.

the championship math shifted. Norris now leads by nine points since Piastri's DNF basically handed those points away. Oscar fell to within 30 of Verstappen. so Norris ahead by a tiny margin, Piastri suddenly vulnerable, Verstappen lurking. the classic three-way nightmare for anyone trying to predict what happens next.

honestly, brazil didn't deliver much on the weather front (the forecast lied). but the sprint itself was genuinely entertaining in the dumbest ways possible (aquaplaning cars, mystery LICO, Lawson incidents). the main race forecast looks dry, which means we're probably looking at a McLaren procession since they've been faster in the dry all weekend.

the race starts at 20:00 here in riyadh, so catch you for the grand prix summary.
