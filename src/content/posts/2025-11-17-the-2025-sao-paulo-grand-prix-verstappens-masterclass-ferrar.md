são paulo is genuinely the best race on the calendar. rain, crashes, drama, and it's always critical for the wdc. this weekend had all of it. except the rain this time (still entertaining though). max verstappen started from the pitlane and finished on the podium. lando norris is now leading the championship by 24 points. ferrari won the drivers' parade and managed absolutely nothing in the actual race. let's talk about what happened.

qualifying was a statement moment for norris. pole position. again. this is his weekend now. kimi antonelli took p2 (looking sharp all year), charles leclerc p3, oscar piastri p4. the real story though: max verstappen. q1 exit. his first one in ten years on pure pace. no grip. which meant red bull had to make the call: pit lane start with upgraded parts, or start p16 with old machinery. they chose the pit lane. smart call, honestly. max looked like he was going to make them regret it.

the drivers' parade was peak chaos (in the good way). classic 1960s cars, and williams absolutely won it. alex albon and carlos sainz realized only one of them could drive, so they footraced across the track like children. albon won and got shotgun. then both took a shortcut through the circuit since why not. aston martin's car broke so they hitched a ride with mclaren. ferrari won the parade. cute. didn't matter for later.

race start came and went clean. then gabriel bortoleto, the home hero, crashed lap one. dnf. his own fans watched him crash twice in the same weekend (sprint and race). brutal. lewis hamilton then ripped his front wing trying to overtake franco colapinto. damage to the chassis. he was out. the safety car came and went quickly.

restart was where it got messy. piastri had a better exit than norris and dove to turn 1. antonelli didn't leave space. contact. piastri went into leclerc like bumper cars. leclerc's front left was destroyed. dnf. both ferraris out within laps of each other. their parade win was their whole weekend.

the fia gave piastri a 10-second penalty. i think antonelli didn't leave room and that's racing, but the stewards disagreed with me (shocking). moving on.

max meanwhile made it into the top 10 by lap 20. then a puncture forced him to pit. seemed like it was over. by the final 20 laps though, he was hunting podium positions. did we expect anything less? turn 1 overtake on george russell. aggressive. clean. then he chased down antonelli in p2. couldn't get him before the line.

norris took the win. antonelli held p2 (his second career podium). max stormed from the pitlane to p3. from dead last to podium. that's not a masterclass, that's just embarrassing everyone else.

standings-wise, norris extended his lead to 24 points over verstappen. piastri is now 25 points behind norris, which is absolutely mental since he led the championship in april. this is the "norris era" everyone's been talking about. the back half of the season has been a demolition job by him on piastri. piastri had a mediocre second half while norris figured out the mclaren since austin. that's the championship right there.

next weekend in las vegas, max has to win. if norris finishes p3 or higher and max doesn't win, max gets mathematically eliminated. so basically: max wins, he stays alive. max doesn't, his season is done. that's pressure.

catch you in vegas.
