alright, so i was supposed to write a vegas summary. that's not happening (semester exams immediately after qatar, and apparently those matter more than f1 content to my father, which is objectively wrong). but here's the trade-off: i spent friday at lusail with paddock club access, which means i have a lot of ridiculous stories and some photos that prove i was actually there.

first things first. the moment you walk in, they put a lanyard on you and the porsche carrera cup is so absurdly loud you immediately understand why the aston martin earplugs they gave us aren't a joke. this building sits directly over the pit lane. you're not separated from the sport. you're basically living in it.

the pit lane walk happens first. and yes, i got photos of every garage (including staring directly at mercedes' setup while kimi antonelli walked out to talk to his engineers). signature number one. then you walk the grid, see the five red lights, and since we were hosted by aston martin, we got to stand outside their garage and take photos with an actual car. which is insane when you think about it. most people watch this on a screen.

inside the paddock club itself there's food (the beef tenderloin on friday was genuinely incredible), free simulators, and the viewing terrace on the top floor. this terrace is absurdly high up. you can see the entire track. fp1 starts and the noise is so overwhelming that the cars aren't just fast in the way you see on tv. they're something else entirely. the wheel guns during pit stops might be louder than the engines. seriously. i had to wear earplugs to stand outside without feeling like my ears were collapsing.

15 minutes left in fp1 and our hosts take us down to the paddock itself. this is where it gets ridiculous. since drivers were just exiting their garages, i got signatures from yuki tsunoda (first signature, red bull polo), liam lawson, valtteri bottas, helmut marko, flavio briatore, lando norris (who signed my shirt), oscar piastri, and mari boya. i was apparently being filmed by lusail circuit's social media team while chasing lando around the paddock, which is either embarrassing or the best thing that's ever happened to me. possibly both.

then sprint qualifying. right before it starts, i'm on the bridge above aston martin's garage entrance and both fernando alonso and lance stroll walk in. stroll jogs. this matters for reasons that will become clear in about five minutes.

sq1 happens and lewis hamilton gets knocked out in p17. lance stroll, driving a demonstrably slower car, outqualifies the greatest driver in the history of motorsport. to absolutely no one's surprise, the kids around me are losing their minds. lance can't hear them since he's wearing airpods. which is funny.

sq2 is forgettable except isack hadjar (the rookie with the best qualifying record) doesn't make it out. alonso makes it through. so does piastri, russell, norris, and tsunoda.

then our hosts do something ridiculous. they bring us into aston martin's garage itself. into the viewing gallery. we're standing there with radios, listening to alonso's engineer talk to him in real time, watching both aston cars get boxed directly in front of us. sq3 starts and alonso puts in a lap that's absolutely pristine. p4. the entire garage loses it. not because p4 is world-shattering, but because the car finally showed up. piastri took pole (no surprises there), russell p2, norris p3.

here's the thing that matters though. tsunoda outqualified max verstappen. and if we're being honest, alonso outqualified lance in every single session. which means alonso was the only driver on the entire grid to beat his teammate in every qualifying segment of the weekend. in 2025. on a team that fights like cats and dogs for development priority. draw whatever conclusions you want from that.

i thought friday was going to be the peak of the weekend. standing in a garage, hearing radio chatter, watching engineers celebrate a decent qualifying result. but apparently i was wrong. apparently saturday and sunday got even more absurd.

see you at the next race.
