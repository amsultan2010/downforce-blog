there's a thing that happens in F1 sometimes where one team gets so far ahead that the narrative just sort of... deflates. the championship becomes inevitable, the dominance becomes background noise, and everyone starts looking for side stories to stay interested.

mercedes is 72 points clear of ferrari in the constructor standings with 13 races still to go. that's not a championship race anymore. that's a procession. and yet we're all sitting here obsessing over whether max verstappen will somehow claw back into relevance (he won't), or if mclaren's strategy department will finally make a correct decision (they might), or if we'll see some proper drama at monaco tomorrow.

nobody's really talking about how completely and utterly mercedes has dismantled the field.

the actual story is andrea kimi antonelli, obviously. 131 points. he's lapping everyone not in a silver car. george russell is his closest rival at 88, but that gap keeps growing. leclerc and hamilton are both genuinely talented, talented-on-the-track drivers doing everything right and still losing 50+ points because their machinery just isn't in the same conversation. oscar piastri and lando norris are having a season that would be dominant in basically any other year and they're fighting over crumbs.

and max verstappen (the guy who was supposed to be unbeatable, the guy we spent three years being told had transcended the sport) is in seventh place, 88 points down, probably done with his world championship hopes before wimbledon.

if you were watching f1 in 2014, this feels familiar in exactly the wrong way.

here's what's wild though: antonelli is doing this as a rookie (or effectively a rookie since he basically sat out last year). he's not making rookie mistakes. he's not struggling in the car. he's just... dominating. the kid is 22, he's in his second season properly, and he's built a championship lead that would make any driver look comfortable. mercedes went all-in on him and it's paid off in a way that's almost boring to watch (except it's not boring when you're from the gulf and you're watching at 3am thinking "wait, is this kid actually just better than everyone else right now").

the constructor championship was never in doubt, but it's still worth acknowledging: mercedes got this right. they called kimi up, they gave him the tools, and he's repaid them with near-flawless execution. russell is there as the seasoned number two, solid, dependable, and utterly outmatched by his teammate. that's not an indictment of george. it's just what happens when you put a generational talent in a good car.

which brings us to tomorrow at monaco.

street circuits should theoretically be leveling grounds. narrow margins, tight corners, less about raw performance and more about precision. except when one team is 72 points clear, precision doesn't matter as much. ferrari might beat mercedes on the day. leclerc always shows up at monaco. but it won't change the underlying reality: mercedes will win the championship. antonelli will almost certainly win the world championship. the race for second through eighth is more meaningful than the fight for first.

that's not speculation. that's just what the points say.

the question now isn't whether mercedes wins. it's whether this dominance stays interesting long enough to keep us watching.

(spoiler: it will, since we're all contractually obligated to care about f1 until the end of time.)
