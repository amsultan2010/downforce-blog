monaco happened and somehow the most dominant car all season stayed dominant at a circuit that usually punishes you for being too dominant. kimi antonelli just won the principality. the kid is now 156 points clear. let that sink in. we're not even halfway through the season and the championship is already feeling less like a competition and more like a coronation.

the setup was straightforward going in. antonelli had the best car, the best form, and the pole position stakes were absurdly high at a track where qualifying essentially decides everything (or used to, anyway). ferrari needed to deliver at home. red bull needed verstappen to do something genius. mclaren had lando norris fighting for relevance after a rough few rounds. none of that happened the way anyone expected.

qualifying didn't tell us much because anton looked untouchable. the mercedes was just faster. the usual suspects were somewhere behind him, and then there was a whole lot of car park. nothing surprising there.

the race is where it got interesting (in the sense that "interesting" means "a lot of people retired").

antonelli led from the start and basically never looked stressed about it. he drove the clean line, managed his tires, and let everyone else fight over the scraps. lewis hamilton finished second for ferrari, which is the kind of silverware the scuderia needed after charles leclerc retired. leclerc going out at monaco is peak ferrari energy (they do this almost every year, right?). carlos sainz also retired, though that's a williams problem, not a ferrari one. sainz hasn't been at ferrari since 2024. you'd almost feel bad if it wasn't so predictable.

max verstappen? retired. lando norris? retired. neither of them finished. this is what monaco does sometimes. it chews people up and spits them out. the weather was clean, the circuit was fine, so it was pure driver error or mechanical gremlin. verstappen's retirement in particular feels relevant since he's now sitting on just 43 points in the championship. the dominance is gone. something shifted.

isack hadjar brought the red bull home in third. not bad for a team that's clearly lost its edge this season. oscar piastri managed fourth for mclaren, which is solid considering mclaren's strategy calls have been historically iffy (they didn't blow it this weekend, amazingly). liam lawson finished fifth for rb, arvid lindblad sixth, same team. gasly got seventh for alpine. albon eighth for williams. ocon ninth for haas. and then there's fernando alonso in tenth for aston martin (genuinely one of the better stories of the weekend). aston's car has been a disaster all season and alonso dragged it into the points anyway. first championship point for the team in 2026. sergio perez was classified tenth on the road but got a post-race penalty for a false start, which bumped him out of the points and handed alonso the position. the chairman collects.

the real storyline here is antonelli's stranglehold on the championship. he's 66 points clear of lewis hamilton. 68 ahead of george russell. the gap is genuinely cartoonish. mercedes has built something special this season and antonelli is executing at a level that makes it look easy. whether he'll maintain this through barcelona and beyond is the only question worth asking at this point.

hamilton's second place finish is interesting since he's now firmly in the championship fight behind antonelli, and that's a dynamic we'll see play out over the next rounds. can he capitalize on any mercedes miscues or antonelli mistakes? probably not. but being 66 points back is different from being 100 back, so the narrative isn't completely dead yet (even if the math kind of is).

the real tragedy is ferrari fumbling at home with leclerc out. one car finished on the podium (hamilton), one didn't finish at all. the tifosi deserved better. they always do, and they never get it.

for barcelona, expect antonelli to extend that lead even further. the kid's in a different league right now. hamilton might scrape a podium. verstappen needs to find whatever he lost. piastri will be competitive but mclaren will find a way to make it complicated. hadjar will be solid. the rb boys will fight. this is the script we're living in now.

see you in barcelona.
